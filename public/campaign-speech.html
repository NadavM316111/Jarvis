DIRECTOR OF SIMULATIONS CAMPAIGN SPEECH

Nadav L. Minkowitz

[Walk to the center. Pause. Make eye contact. Then begin slowly.]

"Fellow delegates..."

[Pause for 2 seconds]

"I want you to think back to your first day in Model UN."

[Let that sit]

"Maybe you walked in not knowing what a placard was. Maybe you didn't know the difference between a moderated and unmoderated caucus. Maybe... you were terrified to raise your hand."

[Slight pause, then shift tone - warmer]

"I remember that feeling. We ALL remember that feeling."

[Now build energy]

"But then something happened. Someone - a chair, a fellow delegate, a simulation that actually MATTERED to you - made you WANT to speak. Made you WANT to be heard. Made you realize that YOUR voice... has power."

[Pause. Point to the audience]

"THAT is what Model UN is supposed to be."

[Now pivot - get serious]

"But here's the truth..."

[Lower voice slightly]

"Not every simulation does that. Some topics feel disconnected. Some background guides collect dust. Some sessions... we just go through the motions."

[Build back up with conviction]

"I'm running for Director of Simulations because I REFUSE to let that be our club."

[Stronger now]

"I have been in committee for TWO years. I know the parliamentary procedure. I know how to write a resolution. But more importantly - I know what makes a delegate lean forward in their chair. I know what makes someone who's NEVER spoken... finally raise their placard."

[Pause - deliver the vision]

"If you elect me, here is my promise:"

"ONE - Every topic will be something you actually CARE about. Issues that affect YOUR generation. YOUR future. Not some dusty debate from 1987."

"TWO - New delegates will feel WELCOME, not overwhelmed. I will build simulations that teach while they challenge."

"THREE - I will bring NHSMUN-level quality... right here... to THIS lecture hall."

[Slow down for the close - make it personal]

"I don't have a shelf full of certificates."

[Pause]

"But I have something that matters more."

[Hand on chest]

"Dedication. Work ethic. And a genuine belief that EVERY person in this room... deserves a simulation worth caring about."

[Final beat - look across the room]

"Model UN changed me. Let me help it change YOU."

[Confident, calm]

"My name is Nadav Minkowitz. I'm asking for your vote for Director of Simulations."

[Nod once]

"Thank you."

[Walk back. Don't rush.]

DELIVERY NOTES

RHETORICAL TECHNIQUES:

  • Anaphora (repetition): "Maybe you... Maybe you... Maybe you..."
  • Rule of Three: Three promises, delivered clearly
  • Contrast: "I don't have certificates... BUT I have..."
  • Direct address: "YOUR voice... YOUR generation... YOUR future"
  • Emotional arc: Nostalgia - Problem - Solution - Personal - Call to action
  • Strategic pauses: Let silence do the work (MLK's signature)

TIMING: ~2 minutes. Perfect length.

KEY TIP: Speak SLOWER than feels natural. Let the pauses breathe.
