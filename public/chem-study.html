1. Chemical Equation Anatomy

Reactants are the starting substances (LEFT side of the arrow). Products are what's formed (RIGHT side of the arrow).

REACTANTS ---yields---> PRODUCTS

2H2(g) + O2(g) ---> 2H2O(l)

States of Matter Symbols:

  • (s) = solid
  • (l) = liquid
  • (g) = gas
  • (aq) = aqueous (dissolved in water)

2. BrINClHOF Twins - The Diatomic 7

Memory Trick: "BrINClHOF" - Bromine, Iodine, Nitrogen, Chlorine, Hydrogen, Oxygen, Fluorine
These 7 elements ALWAYS come in pairs when alone in nature!
Br2, I2, N2, Cl2, H2, O2, F2

When writing equations, if any of these elements appear alone (not in a compound), they MUST have a subscript of 2!

3. Subscripts vs Coefficients

2H2O
  • Coefficient (yellow 2) = Number BEFORE the formula. Changes how many molecules. CAN be changed when balancing.
  • Subscript (cyan 2) = Number AFTER an element, slightly lower. Shows atoms in the compound. CANNOT be changed when balancing!
Remember: Changing a subscript changes the IDENTITY of the compound. Changing a coefficient changes the AMOUNT.

4. Writing Equations from Words

Steps to convert word equations:

  • 1. Identify all reactants and products
  • 2. Write correct chemical formulas for each
  • 3. Remember BrINClHOF for diatomic elements
  • 4. Use proper state symbols
"Hydrogen gas reacts with oxygen gas to produce water"

H2(g) + O2(g) ---> H2O(l)

5. Balancing Equations

Rules for balancing:

  • 1. Count atoms of each element on both sides
  • 2. Add COEFFICIENTS (never change subscripts!)
  • 3. Start with the most complex molecule
  • 4. Save H and O for last
  • 5. Double-check your work!
UNBALANCED: H2 + O2 ---> H2O
H: 2 = 2 (good) | O: 2 != 1 (bad)

BALANCED: 2H2 + O2 ---> 2H2O
H: 4 = 4 | O: 2 = 2

6. Five Reaction Types

1. Synthesis (Combination)

Two or more substances combine to form ONE product

A + B ---> AB
2Na + Cl2 ---> 2NaCl

2. Decomposition

ONE compound breaks down into two or more simpler substances

AB ---> A + B
2H2O ---> 2H2 + O2

3. Single Replacement (Displacement)

One element replaces another in a compound

A + BC ---> AC + B
Zn + 2HCl ---> ZnCl2 + H2

4. Double Replacement (Displacement)

Two compounds exchange partners

AB + CD ---> AD + CB
NaCl + AgNO3 ---> NaNO3 + AgCl

5. Combustion

A substance reacts with O2, producing CO2 and H2O (for hydrocarbons)

Hydrocarbon + O2 ---> CO2 + H2O
CH4 + 2O2 ---> CO2 + 2H2O

7. Activity Series (Single Replacement)

CEO Analogy: A more active metal (higher on the activity series) can "kick out" a less active metal from its compound - like a CEO taking someone's job!
Most Active (Top)Least Active (Bottom)
Li, K, Ba, Ca, NaThese can replace H from water & acids
Mg, Al, Zn, Fe, NiThese can replace H from acids only
Sn, Pb, HH is the reference point
Cu, Hg, Ag, Pt, AuCannot replace H - too weak!
Zn + CuSO4 ---> ZnSO4 + Cu (GOES! Zn is above Cu)
Cu + ZnSO4 ---> NO REACTION (Cu is below Zn)

8. Double Replacement & Precipitation

A precipitate is an insoluble solid that forms from a reaction in solution.

Solubility Rules (Simplified):

SOLUBLE (dissolves)INSOLUBLE (precipitates)
All Group 1 (Na+, K+, Li+) compoundsMost carbonates (CO3)
All nitrates (NO3-)Most phosphates (PO4)
All ammonium (NH4+) compoundsMost hydroxides (OH) except Group 1
Most chlorides, bromides, iodidesSilver halides (AgCl, AgBr, AgI)

9. Complete, Total Ionic & Net Ionic Equations

Complete Equation (CE)

Shows all reactants and products as complete formulas

NaCl(aq) + AgNO3(aq) ---> NaNO3(aq) + AgCl(s)

Total Ionic Equation (TIE)

Break apart all aqueous (aq) compounds into ions. Keep solids, liquids, and gases together!

Na+(aq) + Cl-(aq) + Ag+(aq) + NO3-(aq) ---> Na+(aq) + NO3-(aq) + AgCl(s)

Net Ionic Equation (NIE)

Remove spectator ions (ions that appear unchanged on both sides)

Ag+(aq) + Cl-(aq) ---> AgCl(s)
Spectator Ions: Na+ and NO3- are spectators - they watch but don't participate! They appear identical on both sides.

MOST LIKELY TEST QUESTIONS

1. Balance This Equation & Classify

You'll likely see: "Balance the following equation and identify the reaction type"

__Fe + __O2 ---> __Fe2O3

Answer: 4Fe + 3O2 ---> 2Fe2O3 (Synthesis)

2. Word Equation to Balanced Formula

Example: "Magnesium metal reacts with hydrochloric acid to produce magnesium chloride and hydrogen gas"

Mg(s) + 2HCl(aq) ---> MgCl2(aq) + H2(g)
Type: Single Replacement

3. Activity Series - Will It React?

Example: "Will copper react with silver nitrate solution?"

Cu + AgNO3 ---> ?
YES! Cu is above Ag in activity series
Cu + 2AgNO3 ---> Cu(NO3)2 + 2Ag

4. Write CE, TIE, and NIE

Example: "Sodium sulfate reacts with barium chloride"

CE: Na2SO4(aq) + BaCl2(aq) ---> 2NaCl(aq) + BaSO4(s)

TIE: 2Na+ + SO4^2- + Ba^2+ + 2Cl- ---> 2Na+ + 2Cl- + BaSO4(s)

NIE: Ba^2+(aq) + SO4^2-(aq) ---> BaSO4(s)

Spectator Ions: Na+, Cl-

5. Identify Precipitate Using Solubility Rules

Example: "What precipitates when mixing lead(II) nitrate and potassium iodide?"

Pb(NO3)2 + 2KI ---> PbI2 + 2KNO3

PbI2 is INSOLUBLE (lead iodide precipitates as yellow solid)
KNO3 is SOLUBLE (all nitrates are soluble)

6. BrINClHOF Application

Example: "Write the balanced equation for hydrogen reacting with nitrogen to form ammonia"

Remember: H2 and N2 (both are diatomic!)

3H2(g) + N2(g) ---> 2NH3(g)
Type: Synthesis

7. Combustion Reactions

Example: "Write the balanced combustion equation for propane (C3H8)"

C3H8 + 5O2 ---> 3CO2 + 4H2O

All hydrocarbon combustion: CxHy + O2 ---> CO2 + H2O
