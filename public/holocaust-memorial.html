The Descent into Darkness

1933

Rise of the Nazi Party

Adolf Hitler becomes Chancellor of Germany. The Nazi regime begins systematic persecution of Jews, stripping them of citizenship, rights, and dignity through the Nuremberg Laws.

1938

Kristallnacht

The "Night of Broken Glass" - Nazi forces destroy thousands of Jewish businesses, synagogues, and homes. 30,000 Jewish men are sent to concentration camps. The violence marks the beginning of the Holocaust.

1941

The Final Solution

Nazi leadership implements the systematic genocide of European Jews. Death camps are constructed across occupied Poland. Mass shootings by Einsatzgruppen murder over 1.5 million Jews.

1945

Liberation

Allied forces liberate the concentration camps, revealing the full horror of the Holocaust to the world. Six million Jews - two-thirds of European Jewry - have been murdered.

"We must always take sides. Neutrality helps the oppressor, never the victim. Silence encourages the tormentor, never the tormented." - Elie Wiesel, Nobel Peace Prize Acceptance Speech, 1986

Timeline of Tragedy

1933

Dachau, the first concentration camp, opens. Book burnings begin across Germany.

1939

World War II begins. Jews in Poland are forced into ghettos.

1942

Wannsee Conference formalizes the "Final Solution." Auschwitz-Birkenau becomes primary death camp.

1943

Warsaw Ghetto Uprising - Jewish resistance fighters hold off Nazi forces for nearly a month.

1945

Auschwitz liberated January 27th. Germany surrenders May 8th. The world confronts the Holocaust.
