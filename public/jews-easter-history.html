THE HIDDEN HISTORY

Exploring the dark connection between Easter celebrations and the persecution of Jewish communities throughout history

The Easter-Passover Connection

THE ORIGINS

Passover and Easter

Easter and Passover occur around the same time, creating a historical intersection that often turned deadly for Jewish communities. The Christian Holy Week, commemorating the crucifixion, frequently sparked violence against Jews who were falsely blamed for the death of Jesus.

MEDIEVAL EUROPE

Blood Libel Accusations

One of the most horrific accusations was the "blood libel" - the false claim that Jews murdered Christian children to use their blood in Passover rituals. This baseless lie led to countless massacres, particularly during the Easter season.

RECURRING VIOLENCE

Easter Pogroms

For centuries, Jewish communities lived in fear as Easter approached. Mobs, often incited by religious sermons, would attack Jewish neighborhoods, burning synagogues and homes. These "Easter pogroms" occurred across Europe from Spain to Russia.

"The Jews are not hated because they have evil qualities; evil qualities are found in them because they are hated."

Timeline of Persecution

1096 CE

First Crusade Massacres

Crusaders en route to the Holy Land massacred Jewish communities in the Rhineland. Thousands were killed in Mainz, Worms, and Cologne during the spring season.

1144 CE

First Blood Libel

In Norwich, England, Jews were accused of murdering a Christian boy named William before Easter. This marked the first recorded blood libel accusation.

1190 CE

York Massacre

The entire Jewish community of York was massacred during the Easter season. 150 Jews took refuge in Clifford's Tower before choosing death over forced conversion.

1290 CE

Expulsion from England

King Edward I expelled all Jews from England, following decades of persecution and blood libel accusations. They would not return for nearly 400 years.

1492 CE

Spanish Expulsion

The Alhambra Decree expelled all Jews from Spain, ending centuries of Jewish presence. Many who remained and converted (conversos) faced the Inquisition.

1903 CE

Kishinev Pogrom

During Easter in Kishinev (modern Moldova), a devastating pogrom killed 49 Jews and injured hundreds. It was sparked by blood libel accusations in local newspapers.

1933-1945

The Holocaust

The culmination of centuries of antisemitism. Six million Jews were systematically murdered by Nazi Germany, with deep roots in the religious hatred cultivated over centuries.

Understanding the Legacy

MODERN ERA

Church Reconciliation

In 1965, the Second Vatican Council's Nostra Aetate formally repudiated the charge of deicide against Jews. Many Christian denominations have since worked to combat antisemitism and build interfaith bridges.

TODAY

Education and Memory

Holocaust education and remembrance ensure that the lessons of history are not forgotten. Understanding the roots of antisemitism helps prevent its resurgence in new forms.

In Memory

We remember the millions who suffered and died simply because of their faith. Their memory lives on in every candle lit, every prayer spoken, and every act of kindness that defies hatred.
