Early Life

Napoleon Bonaparte was born on August 15, 1769, in Ajaccio, Corsica, just one year after the island was transferred from Genoa to France. Born into a family of minor Italian nobility, he was the fourth of eleven children to Carlo Buonaparte and Letizia Ramolino.

At age nine, Napoleon was sent to mainland France to study at the military academy of Brienne-le-Chateau. Despite being mocked for his Corsican accent and modest background, he excelled in mathematics and history. In 1785, he graduated from the prestigious Ecole Militaire in Paris as a second lieutenant of artillery.

"I am the successor, not of Louis XVI, but of Charlemagne." - Napoleon Bonaparte

Rise to Power

Napoleon's military genius first gained attention during the Siege of Toulon in 1793, where his artillery tactics helped recapture the city from British forces. This victory earned him a promotion to brigadier general at just 24 years old.

His Italian Campaign of 1796-1797 established him as one of France's most brilliant military commanders. Leading an underfunded and demoralized army, he won a series of stunning victories against Austrian and Sardinian forces, securing northern Italy for France.

On November 9, 1799 (18 Brumaire), Napoleon seized power in a coup d'etat, overthrowing the Directory and establishing the Consulate. By 1804, he had crowned himself Emperor of the French in a lavish ceremony at Notre-Dame Cathedral.

Famous Battles

Battle of Austerlitz

December 2, 1805

Often called the "Battle of the Three Emperors," Napoleon defeated the combined forces of Russia and Austria, destroying the Third Coalition.

Battle of Jena-Auerstedt

October 14, 1806

A decisive defeat of Prussia that led to French occupation of Berlin and the collapse of the Prussian military.

Battle of Wagram

July 5-6, 1809

One of the largest battles of the Napoleonic Wars, resulting in Austrian defeat and the Treaty of Schonbrunn.

Battle of Waterloo

June 18, 1815

Napoleon's final defeat by the Duke of Wellington and Prussian forces, ending his Hundred Days return to power.

Legacy

Napoleon's influence extends far beyond his military conquests. The Napoleonic Code, his civil law reform, remains the foundation of legal systems in France and over 40 countries worldwide. He modernized education, established the Bank of France, and built infrastructure that still serves France today.
