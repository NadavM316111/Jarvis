The Rise of an Emperor

1769

Birth in Corsica

Born in Ajaccio, just one year after France acquired Corsica from Genoa. A child of revolution, destined for greatness.

1785

Military Commission

Commissioned as a second lieutenant in the artillery. His mathematical genius and strategic mind immediately set him apart.

1799

Coup of 18 Brumaire

Seized power in a dramatic coup, becoming First Consul of France. The Republic was his; the Empire would follow.

1804

Coronation as Emperor

Crowned himself Emperor in Notre-Dame Cathedral, taking the crown from the Pope's hands. "I found the crown of France in the gutter, and I picked it up with my sword."

1812

Russian Campaign

Led 600,000 men into Russia. The Grande Armee met its greatest challenge against the Russian winter and scorched earth tactics.

1821

Death on St. Helena

Died in exile at 51, but his legend had only just begun. "I wished to found a European system, a European Code of Laws, a European Court of Appeal."

Battles of Glory

DECEMBER 2, 1805

Austerlitz

DECISIVE VICTORY

The Battle of the Three Emperors. Napoleon's greatest tactical masterpiece, defeating the combined armies of Russia and Austria.

OCTOBER 14, 1806

Jena-Auerstedt

CRUSHING VICTORY

The Prussian army, heirs of Frederick the Great, destroyed in a single day. Berlin fell within weeks.

JULY 5-6, 1809

Wagram

STRATEGIC VICTORY

The largest battle in history at that time. 300,000 men clashed as Napoleon broke Austrian resistance.

SEPTEMBER 7, 1812

Borodino

PYRRHIC VICTORY

The bloodiest single day of the Napoleonic Wars. 70,000 casualties. Moscow lay open, but victory rang hollow.

OCTOBER 16-19, 1813

Leipzig

DEFEAT

The Battle of Nations. All of Europe united against one man. Even in defeat, he remained defiant.

JUNE 18, 1815

Waterloo

FINAL DEFEAT

The Hundred Days ended on Belgian soil. Wellington and Blucher prevailed, but the legend became immortal.
