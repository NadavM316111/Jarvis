Napoleon Bonaparte

Emperor of the French (1804-1814, 1815)

Biography

Napoleon Bonaparte (1769-1821) was a French military commander and political leader who rose to prominence during the French Revolution and led several successful campaigns during the Revolutionary Wars.


Born in Corsica shortly after its annexation by France, Napoleon was educated at military schools in France. He rapidly rose through the ranks of the military during the French Revolution and became Emperor of the French in 1804.


His military genius and political acumen reshaped the map of Europe and established lasting reforms in law, education, and government that continue to influence the modern world.

"Impossible is a word to be found only in the dictionary of fools."

- Napoleon Bonaparte

Key Events

1769 - Birth

Born on August 15 in Ajaccio, Corsica, just one year after the island was transferred to France from Genoa.

1799 - Coup d'etat

Napoleon seizes power in France through a coup, becoming First Consul and effectively ending the French Revolution.

1804 - Emperor

Crowned Emperor of the French at Notre-Dame Cathedral in Paris, crowning himself in a dramatic ceremony.

1805 - Austerlitz

Wins his greatest victory at the Battle of Austerlitz against Russia and Austria, cementing French dominance.

1812 - Russian Campaign

The disastrous invasion of Russia marks the beginning of Napoleon's downfall, losing over 400,000 soldiers.

1815 - Waterloo

Final defeat at the Battle of Waterloo ends the Napoleonic Wars and leads to his exile to Saint Helena.

1821 - Death

Dies on May 5 on the island of Saint Helena at age 51, likely from stomach cancer.

Famous Battles

Battle of the Pyramids (1798)

Napoleon's Egyptian campaign victory against the Mamluks, showcasing his tactical brilliance with the famous "divisional square" formation.

Battle of Austerlitz (1805)

The "Battle of the Three Emperors" - Napoleon's masterpiece victory against combined Russian and Austrian forces.

Battle of Jena (1806)

Decisive victory over Prussia that effectively destroyed the Prussian army and led to French occupation of Berlin.

Battle of Waterloo (1815)

Napoleon's final battle and defeat by the Duke of Wellington and Prussian forces, ending his rule forever.

Lasting Legacy

⚖

Napoleonic Code

His civil code became the foundation for legal systems in over 40 countries worldwide, establishing principles of equality before the law.

🏫

Education Reform

Established the lycee system and universities that modernized French education and influenced educational systems globally.

🏛

Infrastructure

Built roads, bridges, and canals across Europe. Paris was transformed with new boulevards, monuments, and the Arc de Triomphe.
