PHASE 1: FOUNDATION (Today - Month 3)

Build your digital infrastructure and initial following. This is about establishing credibility and creating shareable content.

Week 1-2: Account Optimization

  • TikTok Bio: "Building America's third party from the ground up | FL | Common ground > common enemies" - Link to Instagram
  • Instagram Bio: "The Phoenix Party | Rising above division | 87% of Americans want term limits. Let's make it happen." - Link to website/Linktree
  • Profile Picture: Your Phoenix logo - consistent across all platforms
  • Create a Linktree: Include petition link, email signup, website, all social platforms

Week 1-4: Content Calendar Launch

TikTok Algorithm Keys: Watch time, completion rate, and engagement velocity. Hook viewers in first 1-2 seconds. Optimal length: 30-90 seconds for political content.
  • Post 3-5x per week on TikTok, 5-7x per week on Instagram
  • Content Pillars:
    - "Did You Know?" stats (87% want term limits, 83% want money out of politics)
    - "Both Sides Agree" - showing consensus issues
    - "Phoenix Explains" - breaking down political concepts simply
    - "Young Voices Matter" - Gen Z perspective on politics
    - Behind-the-scenes of building a party from scratch
  • Trending Audio: Check TikTok Creative Center daily - jump on trends within 24 hours
  • Hashtags: Use specific niche tags (#thirdparty #genzvotes #politicaltiktok) NOT generic #FYP #viral

Month 1-3: Community Building

  • Respond to EVERY comment in first hour after posting
  • Go live weekly on TikTok - "Phoenix Town Hall" format
  • Create Instagram Story polls daily on policy issues
  • Start a Discord server for core supporters
  • Launch email newsletter (free via Substack or Mailchimp)

MONTH 3 GOAL

5,000 TikTok followers | 2,500 Instagram followers | 500 email subscribers | 100 Discord members

PHASE 2: AMPLIFICATION (Months 4-12)

Viral Content Strategy

  • Stitch/Duet political videos - engage with mainstream political content with your third-party perspective
  • Create "Phoenix Response" series - react to political news within hours
  • Collaborate with other young political creators - cross-pollinate audiences
  • Street interview format - "What issue do you think BOTH parties ignore?"
  • Data visualization videos - make statistics visually compelling

Local Media Outreach

  • Write op-eds for South Florida Sun-Sentinel, Miami Herald
  • Pitch story: "15-year-old launches third party movement in Florida"
  • Contact local news stations for interviews
  • Appear on local political podcasts

Build Core Team (Volunteer Network)

  • Recruit via social media: "Join the Phoenix Flock"
  • Key roles needed: Social media manager, content creator, graphic designer, community manager
  • Hold monthly virtual team meetings
  • Create volunteer training materials

YEAR 1 GOAL

25,000 TikTok followers | 15,000 Instagram followers | 3,000 email list | First local media coverage

PHASE 3: POLITICAL INFRASTRUCTURE (Years 2-3)

Legal & Organizational Setup

Register as a Political Committee with Florida Division of Elections
Open campaign bank account (once 18+)
File FEC paperwork if raising federal funds
Consult with election law attorney
Create official party bylaws and platform document

Grassroots Ground Game

  • Launch "Phoenix Chapters" in Florida counties
  • Host in-person meetups in major cities (Miami, Fort Lauderdale, Tampa, Orlando, Jacksonville)
  • Partner with voter registration organizations
  • Attend local government meetings and speak during public comment
  • Build relationships with other third-party organizations (Forward Party, No Labels contacts)

Petition Infrastructure

CRITICAL: For Governor ballot access, you need 139,492 valid signatures OR pay the $5,565 filing fee. Petitions must be submitted by May 11, 2026 for this cycle. For future cycles, start collecting 18+ months early.
  • Train volunteer petition circulators
  • Create digital-to-physical pipeline (collect contact info online, follow up for signatures)
  • Set up petition stations at community events, college campuses
  • Budget: Paid circulators cost $3-8 per valid signature if needed

PHASE 4: FIRST CAMPAIGNS (Years 4-6)

Strategy: Start LOCAL. Win small races first to build credibility, then scale up. A city council win matters more than a failed Congressional run.

Target Races (In Order)

Year 4-5: Local Nonpartisan Races
- City Council
- School Board
- Special Districts (Water Management, Soil & Water)
- These don't require party affiliation and build name recognition
Year 5-6: State Legislature
- Florida State House (smaller districts, lower signature requirements)
- Target competitive or swing districts
- Build coalition with disaffected voters from both parties
Year 6-8: Congressional Campaign
- U.S. House of Representatives
- Focus on FL districts with high independent voter registration
- Qualifying fee: $10,440 (NPA) or petition route
Year 8-10: Governor Campaign
- The ultimate goal
- By now: statewide name recognition, proven track record, substantial donor base
- Filing fee: $5,565 (NPA) or 139,492 signatures

CONTENT PLAYBOOK: WHAT TO POST

High-Performing Political TikTok Formats

  • "Here's what both parties won't tell you..." - Hook that creates curiosity
  • Green screen with news headlines - React to current events
  • "POV: You're explaining [issue] to [audience]" - Educational content
  • Before/after stats - Visual policy comparisons
  • "Storytime: How I started a political party at 15" - Personal narrative
  • Myth vs Reality series - Debunk common misconceptions

Instagram Strategy

  • Stories: Polls, Q&As, behind-the-scenes, countdown to events
  • Feed posts: High-quality graphics with platform positions
  • Highlights: "Platform" "About Us" "Join" "FAQ"

Posting Schedule

  • TikTok optimal times: 7-9 AM, 12-3 PM, 7-11 PM EST
  • Instagram optimal times: 6-9 AM, 12-2 PM, 7-9 PM EST
  • Monday: Week preview / motivational content
