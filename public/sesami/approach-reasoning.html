Approach Reasoning

The logical framework and decision-making process behind Sesami's AI video analysis approach

Core Decision Framework

Every product decision at Sesami follows a consistent reasoning framework based on three principles: User Safety First, Passive Over Active, and Accessible Over Premium.

Principle 1: User Safety First

When in doubt, err on the side of NOT alarming parents. False positives cause unnecessary anxiety and erode trust. We accept higher false negative rates to protect family mental health.

Principle 2: Passive Over Active

Any feature requiring parent action to collect data will fail. New parents are exhausted. If it doesn't work automatically in the background, it's not a real solution.

Principle 3: Accessible Over Premium

Developmental delays don't discriminate by income. Our solution must work for middle-class families, not just affluent early adopters. Software > Hardware.

Decision Tree: Why AI Video Analysis

Question 1: Can we solve this with software only?

Hardware solutions (wearables, smart cribs, mats) require manufacturing, inventory, shipping, and increase user cost.

YES - Video/Audio analysis uses existing cameras

78% of parents already own baby monitors with cameras. We can deliver value through software alone.

Question 2: Video or Audio or Both?

Audio tracks speech/language. Video tracks motor, social, cognitive. Which covers more milestones in months 0-24?

VIDEO - Motor milestones dominate first 18 months

Rolling, sitting, crawling, walking, reaching, grasping - these are visual. Speech milestones start later (babbling at 6mo, words at 12mo).

Question 3: Cloud processing or Edge (on-device)?

Cloud offers better AI accuracy but raises privacy concerns. Edge is private but limited by device processing power.

HYBRID - Pose estimation on edge, analysis in cloud

Baby's raw video never leaves the home. Only anonymized skeleton/movement data goes to cloud for milestone detection.

Question 4: Real-time alerts or Daily/Weekly summaries?

Real-time feels powerful but may overwhelm. Summaries feel less urgent but respect attention.

SUMMARIES with urgent exception alerts

Weekly milestone progress reports. Real-time alerts ONLY for high-confidence (95%+) regression detection.

Step-by-Step Reasoning

1

Start with the User Pain Point

Parents worry about their baby's development but lack tools for continuous, objective monitoring. Pediatric checkups are too infrequent (every 2-3 months) to catch early delays reliably.

2

Identify the Behavioral Insight

Parents don't want MORE work. They're exhausted. Any solution requiring active logging or testing will fail. The solution must work passively, without parent effort.

3

Map Available Data Sources

What data can we passively collect about infant development? Video (baby monitors), audio (monitors), movement (wearables), and location (mats). Video captures the most milestone types.

4

Apply Technology Feasibility Filter

Computer vision has advanced dramatically (2020-2025). Pose estimation, object detection, and behavior recognition now work reliably on consumer cameras. The tech is ready.

5

Address the Privacy Elephant

Baby video is incredibly sensitive. We solve this with edge AI for pose extraction - raw video never leaves the home. Only anonymized skeleton data reaches the cloud.

6

Design for Trust, Not Features

In the medical-adjacent space, trust is everything. We optimize for accuracy over speed, understate rather than overstate findings, and always provide clear action paths.

The Sesami Approach in One Sentence

Use AI to passively analyze video from cameras parents already own, detecting developmental milestones with high specificity, and surfacing only actionable insights that reduce - not increase - parental anxiety.

What This Approach Enables

  • Zero-Hardware Adoption: Parents sign up and connect their existing baby monitor in under 3 minutes
  • Continuous Monitoring: AI watches 24/7 so parents don't have to remember to log milestones
  • Early Detection: Patterns emerge over weeks that single checkups miss
  • Pediatric Integration: Objective data for doctor discussions, not just subjective parental recall
  • Affordable Access: $15/month puts developmental monitoring within reach of most families
  • Scalable Platform: Software-only model means we can reach millions without manufacturing
