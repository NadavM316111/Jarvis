Envisioning the End User

User personas and journey mapping for Sesami

Target User Demographics

Based on our research, Sesami's primary users are millennial and Gen-Z parents who represent 80% of new parents and actively seek technology-driven solutions for childcare. They are data-driven, research-oriented, and willing to invest in premium products that provide peace of mind and actionable insights.

Emily Chen

The Tech-Forward First-Time Mom

Age: 32 Product Manager Bay Area, CA First child: 4 months

"I track everything - my steps, my sleep, my nutrition. Why wouldn't I want the same level of insight into my baby's development?"

Goals

  • Ensure her baby is developing normally
  • Make data-informed parenting decisions
  • Catch any potential issues early
  • Feel confident and prepared at pediatric visits

Frustrations

  • Conflicting advice from online sources
  • Not knowing what's "normal" vs. concerning
  • Limited time with pediatrician
  • Existing monitors only track sleep

Tech Profile

  • Uses Nanit for sleep tracking
  • Tracks feeding in Huckleberry app
  • Member of 3 parenting subreddits
  • Spends $500+/month on baby products

Decision Factors

  • Evidence-based features
  • Premium build quality
  • Strong privacy protections
  • Positive reviews from other parents

Marcus & David Williams

User Journey Map

Following Emily's journey from awareness to advocacy:

1

Awareness

Emily sees a targeted Instagram ad for Sesami while scrolling during a 3am feeding. The messaging about AI-powered developmental tracking catches her attention - this is something her current Nanit doesn't do.

2

Consideration

She reads reviews, watches demo videos, and compares features to her current monitor. The longitudinal tracking and milestone predictions feel like genuine differentiators. She asks about it in her mom group.

3

Purchase

After her pediatrician mentions developmental milestones at the 4-month visit, Emily decides to purchase. The $349 price point is significant but feels justified given the peace of mind promised.

4

Onboarding

Setup takes 20 minutes. The app guides her through calibration and explains what each tracking feature does. She immediately feels more informed about what to look for.

5

Active Use

Daily, Emily checks the app's milestone timeline and watches video clips of key moments. Weekly, she reviews the development summary. The reassurance becomes part of her routine.

6

Advocacy

At her baby's 6-month checkup, Emily shows the pediatrician the developmental summary. The doctor is impressed and asks for more information. Emily recommends Sesami to three friends.

Key User Insights

  • Data-driven reassurance: Users want confirmation that development is on track, not just alerts when something is wrong
  • Shareable summaries: The ability to share reports with pediatricians is highly valued
  • Privacy is paramount: Parents need confidence that video stays private and secure
  • Educational component: Users want to learn what milestones mean, not just whether they're met
  • Community connection: Comparing (anonymized) to other babies provides context and reassurance
