Expert Survey Results

Survey responses from pediatric and developmental health professionals

Survey Methodology

We distributed a comprehensive survey to pediatric healthcare professionals to understand their perspectives on developmental monitoring technology. The survey was conducted online over a 4-week period and received responses from 47 qualified professionals.

Open-Ended Feedback Highlights

"I would love to see a tool that helps parents document what they're observing at home. So often parents say 'something seems off' but can't articulate specifics. Objective data would transform those conversations."

- Pediatrician, 12 years experience

"My biggest concern would be false positives causing unnecessary anxiety. Any tool needs to be carefully calibrated and emphasize that it supplements - not replaces - professional assessment."

- Developmental Pediatrician, 18 years experience

"The early intervention field desperately needs earlier referrals. By the time children reach us, we've often lost months of valuable therapy window. Home monitoring could be a game-changer."

- Pediatric Occupational Therapist, 8 years experience

Concerns & Recommendations from Experts

Top Concerns (by frequency)

  • Parent anxiety: 67% worried about false positives causing unnecessary stress
  • Privacy: 54% emphasized importance of data security for sensitive health information
  • Over-reliance: 42% concerned parents might skip professional visits
  • Accuracy: 38% wanted validation studies before recommending

Recommendations for Product Design

  • Clear messaging that the tool supplements professional care
  • Educational content to help parents understand milestones
  • Exportable reports for sharing with healthcare providers
  • Conservative alerting threshold to minimize false positives
  • Integration with existing pediatric workflows
