End User Profiles

Detailed personas representing our target users, based on research and interview insights.

SC

Sarah Chen

The Anxious First-Time Mom
34 years old | Marketing Manager | Miami, FL | 8-month-old daughter Emma

"I just want to know if she's on track. Every time she doesn't hit a milestone exactly when the app says she should, I spiral into panic mode. Is that normal? Am I crazy?"

- From user interview, November 2024

Background

  • Works remotely as a marketing manager for a tech startup
  • First pregnancy after 2 years of trying, making her extra protective
  • Research-oriented: reads 10+ parenting articles weekly
  • Active in 3 Facebook mom groups for advice and support
  • Partner travels frequently for work, often solo parenting

Goals

  • Know with certainty that Emma is developing normally
  • Catch any potential issues early enough to intervene
  • Feel confident and prepared for pediatrician visits
  • Stop the 3 AM Google searches that fuel her anxiety
  • Enjoy motherhood without constant worry

Pain Points

  • Overwhelmed by conflicting information online
  • Pediatrician visits feel rushed (15 min average)
  • No objective way to track milestones between visits
  • Constant comparison to other babies on social media
  • Guilt about potentially missing warning signs

Tech Behavior

  • Uses 4 baby apps: Wonder Weeks, Huckleberry, Baby Tracker, What to Expect
  • Takes 50+ photos/videos of Emma weekly
  • Comfortable with smart home devices (Nest, Alexa)
  • Already owns Owlet sock for oxygen monitoring
  • Would pay premium for peace of mind ($100-200 range)

What Sarah THINKS

  • "Other babies seem more advanced than Emma"
  • "The pediatrician probably thinks I'm overreacting"
  • "If something's wrong, I need to catch it early"
  • "I wish there was a way to just KNOW she's okay"

What Sarah FEELS

  • Anxious about hitting milestones "on time"
  • Guilty when she can't remember details for the doctor
  • Frustrated by vague answers like "every baby is different"
  • Exhausted from constant vigilance and worry

What Sarah SAYS

  • "Is she rolling over enough?"
  • "My friend's baby was crawling by now"
  • "Should I be worried about this?"
  • "I just need someone to tell me she's normal"

What Sarah DOES

  • Googles development questions at 2 AM
  • Films Emma attempting milestones to show the doctor
  • Asks mom groups for validation and advice
  • Counts and times everything (feeds, poops, awake windows)
MJ

Marcus Johnson

The Efficiency-Focused Dad of Twins
41 years old | Software Engineer | Fort Lauderdale, FL | 11-month-old twin boys

"I need something that works in the background. I'm an engineer - give me the data, show me the trends, and flag anomalies. I don't have time for manual logging with two babies."

- From user interview, November 2024

Background

  • Senior software engineer at a Fortune 500 company
  • Wife is an ER nurse working 12-hour shifts
  • Manages household logistics like a project
  • Data-driven decision maker in all aspects of life
  • Twins conceived via IVF after fertility struggles

Goals

  • Track both twins efficiently without doubling effort
  • Share developmental data easily with wife
  • Get alerts only when something actually needs attention
  • Understand if the twins' different paces are normal
  • Minimize time spent on baby admin tasks

Pain Points

  • Zero time for manual milestone logging
  • Twins developing at different rates causes confusion
  • Existing apps are too basic for his data needs
  • Hard to coordinate care info with wife's schedule
  • Frustrated by lack of comparative analytics

Tech Behavior

  • Early adopter of smart home technology
  • Builds custom automations (Home Assistant, IFTTT)
  • Prefers dashboards and data visualizations
  • Will pay for quality ($200+ if it saves time)
  • Values API access and data export capabilities
EP

Dr. Emily Patel

The Evidence-Based Pediatrician & Mom
42 years old | Pediatrician (15 years) | Boca Raton, FL | 14-month-old son, 6-year-old daughter

"I see 25 patients a day, 15 minutes each. I know we're missing subtle delays because I can't see these kids in their natural environment. If parents could bring me real movement data, it would change everything."

- From user interview, December 2024

Background

  • Board-certified pediatrician with fellowship in developmental medicine
  • Partner at a 5-physician pediatric practice
  • Published researcher on early intervention outcomes
  • Advocate for technology-enabled care
  • Experienced postpartum anxiety with first child

Goals (Professional)

  • Catch developmental delays earlier than current screening allows
  • Access objective, longitudinal patient data
  • Spend appointment time on counseling, not data gathering
  • Reduce over-referrals while catching true positives
  • Empower parents without overwhelming them

Goals (Personal)

  • Balance medical knowledge with normal parental worry
  • Model healthy developmental tracking for other parents
  • Have objective data to quiet her own anxious thoughts
  • Avoid over-analyzing her son like a patient

Pain Points

  • Current screening tools have 20-25% miss rate
  • Parental recall is unreliable for milestone timing
  • Brief visits don't capture natural movement patterns
  • EHR integration is often clunky or nonexistent
  • As a mom, still experiences irrational worry despite expertise
