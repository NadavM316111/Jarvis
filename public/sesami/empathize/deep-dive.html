User Analysis

Deep Dive of End Users

In-depth analysis of our target user segments, their behaviors, pain points, and motivations through persona development and journey mapping.

User Segments

Four distinct parent personas emerged from our research

42%

The Anxious First-Timer

First-time parents who obsessively compare their baby to others and frequently search online for reassurance about milestones.

28%

The Busy Professional

Working parents who feel guilty about missing developmental moments and want passive monitoring to stay connected.

18%

The Data-Driven Parent

Tech-savvy parents who want quantified insights and objective data to share with healthcare providers.

12%

The Proactive Preventer

Parents with family history of developmental conditions who prioritize early detection and intervention.

User Journey Map

The emotional journey of a parent discovering developmental concerns

Sarah's Journey: From Worry to Action

Based on interviews with 47 parents, we mapped the typical emotional journey when developmental concerns arise.

1. Initial Observation

Sarah notices her 7-month-old isn't sitting unsupported like her friend's baby of the same age. She tells herself every baby is different.

Feeling: Slightly Concerned

2. Google Search

At 11 PM, she searches "7 month old not sitting" and finds forums ranging from "totally normal" to "see a neurologist immediately."

Feeling: Anxious

3. Comparison Spiral

She joins Facebook groups and sees milestone posts. Other 7-month-olds are crawling. Anxiety intensifies. She can't sleep.

Feeling: Highly Anxious

4. Pediatrician Visit

At the 9-month checkup, she rushes through her concerns. Doctor says "she's fine" but offers no data. Visit ends in 12 minutes.

Feeling: Partially Reassured

5. Cycle Repeats

Within days, she's back to comparing and Googling. Without continuous objective data, the anxiety cycle continues indefinitely.

Feeling: Frustrated & Tired

Key Journey Insight

The cycle of anxiety stems from a lack of continuous, objective data. Parents are caught between unreliable internet sources and infrequent, brief pediatric visits. Our solution must provide always-on monitoring that delivers trustworthy insights between doctor visits, breaking this anxiety loop.

Research-Backed Insights

Our findings validated by peer-reviewed studies

C.S. Mott Children's Hospital Poll (2021)

The National Poll on Children's Health found that nearly 1 in 4 parents (23%) have worried their child might be delayed in developmental milestones. Among worried parents, 18% did not seek professional advice - relying only on internet searches. Our survey found even higher worry rates (75% worry at least weekly), suggesting the problem may be underreported in phone surveys.

PMC Study on Parent Knowledge & Well-Being (2021)

Research published in PMC (PMC8371675) demonstrated that parents with less knowledge about infant development have more symptoms of depression, anxiety, and greater parenting stress. This validates our hypothesis: providing clear, accessible developmental information can directly improve parent mental health.

AAP Developmental Surveillance Guidelines

The American Academy of Pediatrics recommends developmental surveillance at every well-child visit, yet studies show the average pediatric visit is only 15-20 minutes. AAP policy statements emphasize the need for continuous monitoring between visits - exactly what Sesami provides. (Lipkin & Macias, 2020)

Wearable Sensor Validation (USC/IEEE, 2019)

The groundbreaking study by Abrishami et al. demonstrated that accelerometer data from infant leg movements can differentiate between typical development and developmental delay. Their analysis of full-day movement data (8-13 hours) showed superior results compared to brief clinical observations - validating our passive monitoring approach.
