Approach Reasoning

The decision framework that led us to AI video analysis as the optimal solution.

Home Ideate Approach Reasoning

Decision Tree Analysis

We used a structured decision tree to systematically evaluate each solution against our core requirements. This approach ensured objective, criteria-based selection rather than intuition-driven choices.

Primary Decision Gates

1

Does it require new hardware purchase?

Why this matters: Our survey showed 67% of parents are unwilling to spend $200+ on baby monitoring devices. Hardware requirements create adoption barriers and exclude lower-income families.

PASS: AI Video, App-Only, Audio
FAIL: Smart Sock, Nursery Cam, Play Mat
2

Does it provide objective, measurable data?

Why this matters: Parents want evidence, not opinions. 78% said they would trust a tool more if it showed measurable progress data rather than self-reported checklists.

PASS: AI Video, Audio
FAIL: App-Only (subjective questionnaires)
3

Can it track motor development milestones?

Why this matters: Motor milestones (rolling, sitting, crawling, walking) are the most common parent concerns and the most clinically validated developmental markers.

PASS: AI Video
FAIL: Audio (language only, no motor)

WINNER: AI Video Analysis

Only solution that passed all three primary decision gates: no hardware required, provides objective data, and can track motor development.

Key Tradeoff Analysis

Tradeoff 1: Accuracy vs. Accessibility

Option A: Medical-grade wearable sensors

Highest accuracy (95%+) but costs $300+, requires compliance, excludes most families

VS
Option B: Smartphone video analysis

Good accuracy (85-90%) but zero cost, universal access, no compliance issues

Decision: We chose accessibility. A tool that reaches 10 million families at 85% accuracy helps more children than a perfect tool only 100,000 families can afford.

Tradeoff 2: Continuous vs. Episodic Monitoring

Option A: Always-on nursery camera

24/7 monitoring but major privacy concerns, fixed location, creepy factor

VS
Option B: Parent-initiated video clips

2-3 minute recordings but privacy-preserving, any location, parent in control

Decision: We chose parent control. Weekly 3-minute clips provide enough data for milestone tracking without the privacy concerns of always-on surveillance.

Tradeoff 3: Cloud vs. On-Device Processing

Option A: Full cloud processing

Most powerful AI but videos uploaded to servers, privacy concerns, bandwidth heavy

VS
Option B: Hybrid approach

Pose estimation on-device, only skeleton data sent to cloud, best of both worlds

Decision: Hybrid approach. Parents feel safer knowing raw video never leaves their phone. Skeleton data is anonymous and low-bandwidth.

Tradeoff 4: Comprehensive vs. Focused Scope

Option A: Track everything (motor, language, social, cognitive)

Complete picture but requires multiple input methods, complex UX, longer time to market

VS
Option B: Start with motor + social-emotional via video

Focused scope but single input method, simple UX, faster launch, can expand later

Decision: Start focused. Motor and social-emotional milestones are the most impactful for early intervention. We can add language analysis later via separate audio feature.

Final Approach Validation

We validated our AI Video Analysis approach against our core user needs:

"I want to know if my baby is developing normally"
AI compares movements to age-appropriate norms
"I don't want to buy expensive devices"
Uses existing smartphone camera
"I want objective data, not just my opinion"
AI provides quantified milestone scores
"I want something to show my pediatrician"
Exportable reports in clinical format
"I don't want more anxiety"
Celebration-first design with contextual concerns
"I want to track progress over time"
Historical video analysis and trend visualization

Next Steps: Engineering Phase

With our approach validated, we move to the Engineering phase to define:

  • Technical Architecture - Hybrid on-device/cloud AI pipeline
  • ML Model Design - Training data, milestone detection algorithms
  • Privacy Implementation - On-device pose extraction, data anonymization
  • UI/UX Specifications - Recording flow, dashboard, reports
  • API Design - Pediatrician integration, data export
