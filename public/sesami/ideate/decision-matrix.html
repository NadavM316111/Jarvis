Winner: AI Video Analysis

With a weighted score of 9.05/10, AI Video Analysis emerged as the clear winner. Key factors:

  • Highest accuracy potential - Computer vision can detect subtle movement patterns
  • Zero hardware cost - Uses existing smartphones
  • Completely non-invasive - No wearables or special equipment
  • Maximum scalability - Deployable globally via app stores
  • Strong differentiation - No major competitor offers this approach

Key Decision Insights

App-Only Came Close

Questionnaire-based apps scored 8.35 but lost on accuracy. Parents want objective data, not self-assessment.

Wearables Failed on Compliance

Smart sock scored only 6.00 - parents and babies resist wearables. The "will my baby keep it on?" problem is real.

Hardware = Barrier

Solutions requiring new hardware (camera, mat) face adoption barriers. Smartphone-first wins.

Privacy Trade-off Accepted

Video analysis has privacy considerations, but parents accept this trade-off for accurate developmental insights.
