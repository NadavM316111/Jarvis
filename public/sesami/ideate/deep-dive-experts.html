Our Expert Advisory Panel

We assembled a diverse panel of experts spanning pediatric medicine, artificial intelligence, child development psychology, UX design, and healthcare regulations. Their collective expertise shaped every aspect of Sesami.

Dr. Rachel Martinez, MD, FAAP

Developmental Pediatrician Children's Hospital of Philadelphia

Background & Credentials

  • MD from Johns Hopkins School of Medicine
  • Pediatric residency at Boston Children's Hospital
  • Fellowship in Developmental-Behavioral Pediatrics at CHOP
  • Board certified in Pediatrics and Developmental-Behavioral Pediatrics
  • 15 years clinical experience with 8,000+ developmental assessments

Research Focus

Dr. Martinez's research focuses on early identification of developmental delays, particularly autism spectrum disorder. She has published 23 peer-reviewed papers on screening tools and early intervention outcomes.

Key Contributions to Sesami

  • Defined clinical thresholds for milestone tracking alerts
  • Validated our assessment criteria against ASQ-3 standards
  • Designed the pediatrician report export format
  • Advised on anxiety-aware messaging for parents

"I see 20 babies a day for well-visits. If parents could come in with objective tracking data showing their child's progress over weeks, it would transform those 15-minute visits. We could focus on real concerns instead of vague worries."

Dr. James Chen, PhD

AI/Computer Vision Researcher Stanford Artificial Intelligence Laboratory

Background & Credentials

  • PhD in Computer Science from MIT, specializing in computer vision
  • Postdoctoral research at Google DeepMind
  • Currently Assistant Professor at Stanford
  • Author of 45+ papers with 12,000+ citations
  • Core contributor to OpenPose and MediaPipe frameworks

Research Focus

Dr. Chen specializes in human pose estimation, motion analysis, and applying computer vision to healthcare. His lab developed the first real-time infant pose estimation model for developmental assessment research.

Key Contributions to Sesami

  • Designed the hybrid on-device/cloud AI architecture
  • Recommended MediaPipe for privacy-preserving pose extraction
  • Defined training data requirements for milestone detection models
  • Advised on model accuracy validation methodology

"The beautiful thing about video analysis is you can do it retroactively. Parents already have hundreds of videos of their babies. Train a model right, and you can analyze months of development in minutes."

Dr. Lisa Thompson, PhD

Child Development Psychologist Yale Child Study Center

Background & Credentials

  • PhD in Developmental Psychology from UCLA
  • Clinical fellowship at Yale Child Study Center
  • Author of bestselling book "The First Two Years"
  • 20 years studying infant cognitive and social development
  • Consultant for Sesame Workshop and PBS Kids

Research Focus

Dr. Thompson's research focuses on the interplay between motor development and cognitive growth, particularly how early movement patterns predict later learning outcomes. She pioneered research on video-based autism screening.

Key Contributions to Sesami

  • Expanded scope beyond motor milestones to social-emotional indicators
  • Defined video cues for eye contact, social smiling, and joint attention
  • Created developmental context for each milestone alert
  • Advised on parent education content

"Motor milestones get all the attention - when did they roll over, when did they walk. But social development is equally important and much harder to track. That's where early intervention makes the biggest difference."

Sarah Kim

UX Design Lead - Health & Wellness Former Calm & Headspace

Background & Credentials

  • MFA in Interaction Design from SVA
  • 7 years designing mental health and wellness apps
  • Led design for Calm's sleep stories feature (100M+ users)
  • Previously at IDEO Health practice
  • Speaker at SXSW, Health 2.0, and UX conferences

Design Philosophy

Sarah specializes in "anxiety-aware design" - creating health apps that inform without alarming. Her framework for calm notifications and positive-first feedback has been adopted by major health tech companies.

Key Contributions to Sesami

  • Redesigned dashboard to celebrate achievements first
  • Created weekly digest notification strategy
  • Developed "Calm Parent Mode" for anxiety-prone users
  • Designed milestone celebration animations

"Every health app thinks they need to alert users constantly. The best apps know when NOT to notify. New parents are already sleep-deprived and anxious - respect that."

Michael Roberts, JD, RAC

Healthcare Regulatory Consultant Former FDA Digital Health Reviewer

Background & Credentials

  • JD from Georgetown Law, focus on FDA regulations
  • 10 years at FDA reviewing digital health submissions
  • Regulatory Affairs Certified (RAC)
  • Now advises 30+ digital health startups
  • Author of "Digital Health Regulatory Playbook"

Expertise Area

Michael specializes in Software as a Medical Device (SaMD) classifications and helping digital health companies navigate FDA requirements. He's particularly known for helping companies find regulatory pathways that enable innovation while ensuring safety.

Key Contributions to Sesami

  • Classified Sesami as wellness device (not medical device)
  • Defined compliant marketing language
  • Created regulatory roadmap for future clinical features
  • Advised on data privacy (HIPAA, COPPA) requirements

"You can build an incredibly valuable developmental tracking tool without claiming to diagnose anything. Wellness category lets you move fast, help parents now, and build the evidence base for clinical features later."
