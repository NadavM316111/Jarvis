Brainstorming Session Results

Our team conducted multiple ideation sessions using techniques like SCAMPER, mind mapping, and "How Might We" questions. We generated over 40 initial concepts, which we consolidated into 6 primary technology approaches for evaluation.

Solution 1: AI Video Analysis System

Description: A smartphone app that uses computer vision AI to analyze videos of the baby during natural play. Parents record 2-3 minute clips, and the AI evaluates movement patterns, motor skills, and developmental markers.

How It Works: Machine learning models trained on thousands of infant videos identify key milestones like head control, rolling, sitting, crawling, and walking patterns. The system compares the infant's movements against age-appropriate developmental norms.

Advantages

  • Non-invasive - no wearables required
  • Uses existing hardware (smartphone)
  • Low cost to implement
  • Can analyze historical videos
  • Natural environment assessment
  • Scalable globally via app stores

Challenges

  • Requires good lighting and angles
  • Limited to visual assessment
  • Privacy concerns with video data
  • Model accuracy depends on training data
Feasibility Score: 9.2/10

Solution 2: Smart Wearable Sock

Description: A soft, comfortable sock embedded with IMU (Inertial Measurement Unit) sensors that track leg movements, kick patterns, and activity levels 24/7.

How It Works: Accelerometers and gyroscopes capture movement data continuously. Algorithms analyze kick frequency, symmetry, and patterns to identify potential motor development concerns.

Advantages

  • Continuous monitoring
  • Quantitative movement data
  • Works in any environment
  • Detects asymmetries early

Challenges

  • Requires wearable compliance
  • Battery and charging needs
  • Higher manufacturing cost
  • Limited to lower body tracking
  • Babies may remove it
Feasibility Score: 7.1/10

Solution 3: Smart Nursery Camera

Description: A dedicated AI-powered camera installed in the nursery that continuously monitors the baby and analyzes developmental behaviors during sleep and play.

How It Works: Always-on computer vision processes video feeds locally, identifying developmental milestones and alerting parents to concerning patterns.

Advantages

  • Passive, continuous monitoring
  • No wearables needed
  • Can track sleep patterns too
  • High-quality video capture

Challenges

  • Fixed location limits coverage
  • Higher hardware cost ($200+)
  • Privacy concerns (always-on camera)
  • Installation complexity
  • Only monitors in one room
Feasibility Score: 6.8/10

Solution 4: App-Only Questionnaire System

Description: A mobile app that guides parents through standardized developmental questionnaires (like ASQ-3) with smart reminders and visualization of progress.

How It Works: Parents answer age-appropriate questions about their baby's abilities. The app scores responses, tracks progress over time, and flags potential concerns.

Advantages

  • Lowest development cost
  • Based on validated screening tools
  • No hardware required
  • Easy to update and improve

Challenges

  • Relies on parent observation accuracy
  • Subjective responses
  • No objective measurements
  • Many competing apps exist
  • Less engaging over time
Feasibility Score: 7.5/10

Solution 5: Smart Play Mat

Description: A pressure-sensitive play mat that tracks how babies interact with it - tracking rolling, crawling patterns, time on tummy, and movement progression.

How It Works: Embedded pressure sensors create a heatmap of baby's position and movements over time. AI analyzes patterns to identify developmental progress.

Advantages

  • Natural play environment
  • No wearables needed
  • Tracks tummy time automatically
  • Gamification potential

Challenges

  • High manufacturing cost
  • Limited to mat surface
  • Durability concerns
  • Babies outgrow it quickly
  • Complex hardware development
Feasibility Score: 5.4/10

Solution 6: Audio Analysis System

Description: A system that analyzes baby's vocalizations - coos, babbles, cries, and early words - to track language development and emotional wellbeing.

How It Works: Audio processing AI categorizes vocalizations and tracks progression from reflexive sounds to intentional communication.

Advantages

  • Tracks language development
  • Can detect hearing issues
  • Works passively in background
  • Lower bandwidth than video

Challenges

  • Limited to audio development only
  • Background noise interference
  • Cannot track motor skills
  • Privacy concerns with audio recording
Feasibility Score: 6.2/10

Initial Ranking

Based on our brainstorming session, we ranked solutions by feasibility and potential impact:

  1. AI Video Analysis - Highest feasibility, uses existing tech, non-invasive
  2. App-Only Questionnaire - Lowest cost, but limited objectivity
  3. Smart Wearable Sock - Good data, but wearable compliance issues
  4. Smart Nursery Camera - Good but privacy concerns and cost
  5. Audio Analysis - Limited scope (language only)
  6. Smart Play Mat - High cost, limited use window

We advanced all 6 solutions to the Decision Matrix for rigorous evaluation against our design criteria.
