Expert Consultation Process

We consulted with 5 domain experts across pediatrics, AI/ML, child development, product design, and regulatory affairs. Their feedback drove 8 major iterations to our design.

Expert 1: Dr. Rachel Martinez - Developmental Pediatrician

Background: 15 years practicing developmental pediatrics at Children's Hospital. Specializes in early intervention referrals.

Consultation Date: January 15, 2026 (45 minutes)

Before Feedback

Our initial design flagged any deviation from CDC milestone averages as "concerning"

After Iteration

Added age-adjusted ranges with clear "normal variation" vs "consult recommended" thresholds based on clinical standards

"The worst thing you can do is create anxious parents who flood pediatric offices with normal babies. Show them what's actually concerning vs what's just individual variation."

- Dr. Rachel Martinez

Before Feedback

App generated generic "milestone reports" without clinical context

After Iteration

Created pediatrician-specific export format aligned with ASQ-3 screening tool language that doctors recognize

Expert 2: Dr. James Chen - AI/ML Research Scientist

Background: Computer vision researcher at Stanford AI Lab. Published work on pose estimation and infant movement analysis.

Consultation Date: January 22, 2026 (60 minutes)

Before Feedback

Planned to require parents to record specific poses/activities on demand

After Iteration

Switched to passive analysis of natural play videos - AI identifies relevant movements automatically

Before Feedback

Cloud-only processing for all video analysis

After Iteration

Hybrid approach: pose estimation on-device, milestone analysis in cloud. Reduces privacy concerns and bandwidth.

"MediaPipe pose estimation is good enough for infant tracking now. Run it locally, send only the skeleton data to your servers. Parents will trust you more."

- Dr. James Chen

Expert 3: Dr. Lisa Thompson - Child Development Specialist

Background: Child psychologist specializing in early childhood development. Author of "The First Two Years" parenting guide.

Consultation Date: February 3, 2026 (40 minutes)

Before Feedback

Focus only on motor milestones (rolling, sitting, walking)

After Iteration

Added social-emotional indicators: eye contact, social smiling, response to name - detectable via video

"Motor delays are important, but autism spectrum indicators show up in social behaviors first. If you can detect reduced eye contact or lack of social smiling, that's incredibly valuable for early intervention."

- Dr. Lisa Thompson

Expert 4: Sarah Kim - UX Design Lead (Health Apps)

Background: Former design lead at Calm and Headspace. Specializes in anxiety-aware health app design.

Consultation Date: February 10, 2026 (50 minutes)

Before Feedback

Dashboard showed "risk scores" and "delay indicators" prominently

After Iteration

Redesigned to celebrate achievements first. "Concerns" only shown when actionable, with clear next steps.

Before Feedback

Push notifications for every analysis result

After Iteration

Weekly summary digests with celebration-first framing. Urgent alerts only for significant findings.

"New parents are already anxious enough. Your app needs to be a source of confidence, not another thing to worry about. Lead with what's going well."

- Sarah Kim

Expert 5: Michael Roberts - Healthcare Regulatory Consultant

Background: Former FDA reviewer, now consults on digital health product compliance. Expert in SaMD (Software as Medical Device) regulations.

Consultation Date: February 18, 2026 (55 minutes)

Before Feedback

Marketing positioned app as "diagnostic tool" for developmental delays

After Iteration

Repositioned as "developmental tracking and awareness tool" - not diagnostic. Avoids FDA Class II medical device requirements.

"Stay in the wellness category. The moment you claim to diagnose anything, you're a medical device and looking at 2+ years and $500K+ for FDA clearance. You can still provide immense value without crossing that line."

- Michael Roberts

Summary of Design Iterations

1

Anxiety-aware messaging - Lead with positives, contextualize concerns

2

Clinical threshold calibration - Use validated age-adjusted ranges

3

Hybrid processing model - On-device pose estimation for privacy

4

Natural play analysis - No structured recording requirements

5

Social-emotional indicators - Beyond just motor milestones

6

Pediatrician export format - ASQ-3 compatible reports

7

Wellness positioning - Avoid medical device classification

8

Weekly digest notifications - Reduce alert fatigue
