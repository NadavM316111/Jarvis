Potential Solutions to Solve Our Problem

Exploring every possible approach to passive infant developmental monitoring before selecting our winning concept

The Core Problem We're Solving

Parents lack accessible, continuous tools to monitor their infant's developmental progress. Current methods rely on periodic pediatric checkups (typically every 2-3 months) and subjective parental observation. Developmental delays affecting 15% of children often go undetected until age 4+, missing critical early intervention windows.

Solution Requirements

  • Must be passive (no active parent involvement required for data collection)
  • Must be non-invasive and safe for infants 0-24 months
  • Must track motor, cognitive, and social developmental milestones
  • Must provide actionable insights, not just raw data
  • Must integrate with existing parenting routines
  • Must be affordable for middle-class families (under $300 total cost)

All Solutions Explored

Our team generated 127 unique ideas across 8 brainstorming sessions. After initial screening, we narrowed to 6 viable technology approaches for deep evaluation.

AI-Powered Video Analysis

Computer vision algorithms analyze footage from existing baby monitors to detect developmental milestones automatically. Uses pose estimation, movement pattern recognition, and behavioral analysis.

Advantages
  • Uses existing hardware (baby monitors)
  • Completely passive monitoring
  • Rich visual data for milestone detection
  • Can track motor, social, and cognitive cues
  • Scalable cloud-based processing
Challenges
  • Privacy concerns with video data
  • Requires good camera positioning
  • Limited to monitored areas only
  • Processing costs for video AI
Feasibility: 92%

Wearable Smart Sock/Anklet

IMU sensors embedded in soft fabric track leg movements, kick patterns, and motor development. Similar to Owlet but focused on development vs. vitals.

Advantages
  • Continuous 24/7 monitoring
  • Precise movement data
  • Works anywhere baby goes
  • No camera privacy concerns
Challenges
  • Hardware manufacturing required
  • Parents must remember to put it on
  • Battery life limitations
  • Higher unit cost ($150-200)
  • Only tracks motor development
Feasibility: 68%

Smart Play Mat with Sensors

Pressure-sensitive mat tracks where baby moves, reaches, and interacts. Embedded toys provide cognitive stimulation while collecting data.

Advantages
  • Natural play environment
  • Tracks reaching and grasping
  • Can measure tummy time
  • No wearables required
Challenges
  • Limited to mat area only
  • Expensive manufacturing
  • Large physical product
  • Babies outgrow it quickly
Feasibility: 45%

Audio Analysis System

AI analyzes baby sounds, cries, coos, and early babbling to track speech and language development milestones.

Advantages
  • Uses existing baby monitors
  • Less privacy-invasive than video
  • 24/7 passive monitoring
  • Tracks speech development well
Challenges
  • Cannot track motor milestones
  • Background noise interference
  • Limited to audio-based milestones
  • Speech milestones start later (6+ months)
Feasibility: 72%

App-Only Manual Tracking

Mobile app with guided prompts helps parents log and track milestones manually with AI-powered analysis of their entries.

Advantages
  • No hardware costs
  • Easy to develop and deploy
  • Works on any smartphone
  • Low technical barriers
Challenges
  • Not passive - requires parent effort
  • Subjective parent observations
  • Low engagement over time
  • Misses what parents don't notice
Feasibility: 95%

Smart Crib with Embedded Sensors

Crib mattress with pressure sensors and side-mounted cameras tracks sleep patterns, movements, and development during rest time.

Advantages
  • Natural monitoring location
  • Combines multiple sensor types
  • Tracks sleep development
  • Always in position
Challenges
  • Very expensive ($500+)
  • Complex hardware integration
  • Safety certifications required
  • Only monitors during sleep/rest
Feasibility: 35%

Why AI Video Analysis Won

After extensive evaluation, AI-powered video analysis emerged as the clear winner for several key reasons:

  • Leverage Existing Hardware: 78% of parents already own baby monitors with cameras. Zero additional hardware purchase required.
  • Comprehensive Tracking: Video captures motor skills, social smiles, eye tracking, reaching, rolling - the full spectrum of visual milestones.
  • True Passive Monitoring: Once set up, parents don't need to do anything. The AI works 24/7 in the background.
  • Scalable Technology: Cloud-based AI processing means the system gets smarter over time with more data.
  • Addressable Privacy: End-to-end encryption, on-device processing options, and transparent data policies can address concerns.
