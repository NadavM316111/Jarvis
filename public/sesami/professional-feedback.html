Expert Feedback Timeline

DK

Dr. Karen Mitchell

Developmental Pediatrician, Children's National Hospital
"Your milestone list is too clinical. Parents don't know what 'palmar grasp reflex integration' means. You need to translate CDC milestones into language parents actually use - 'holding a toy' instead of 'voluntary grasp achieved.'"
Before

Used clinical terminology from ASQ-3 and Bayley Scales directly in parent-facing UI. 156 technical milestone terms.

After

Created "Parent-Friendly Milestone Dictionary" - plain language descriptions with clinical mappings for pediatrician reports. "Baby reaches for toys" maps to "voluntary grasp, midline crossing."

JC

Dr. James Chen

AI/ML Research Lead, Stanford HAI
"Your confidence thresholds are way too aggressive. A 70% confidence alert will generate massive false positive anxiety. For developmental concerns, you need 95%+ confidence before alerting parents, and even then, frame it as 'worth discussing' not 'red flag.'"
Before

Alert parents when AI detects potential delay with 70% confidence. Binary "on track / delayed" status.

After

Three-tier system: Green (95%+ on track), Yellow (discuss at next checkup, 85-95%), Red (schedule appointment, 95%+ delay pattern). No alerts below 85% confidence.

RL

Rachel Lopez, LCSW

Early Intervention Specialist, Florida Early Steps
"You're missing the most critical piece - what do parents DO when they get an alert? Without a clear action path to early intervention services, you're just creating anxiety. Partner with state EI programs directly."
Before

Alert said "Potential delay detected. Consult your pediatrician." No next steps or resources.

After

Built "Action Pathway" feature: Direct links to state Early Intervention programs by ZIP code, pre-filled referral forms, script for pediatrician conversations, and support community connection.

MP

Dr. Maria Patel

Pediatric Neurologist, Boston Children's
"Autism detection claims will get you sued. You cannot and should not attempt to diagnose ASD from video. However, flagging 'patterns worth professional evaluation' for social-communication behaviors is valuable and defensible."
Before

Planned feature: "Early autism risk detection using eye contact and social smile analysis."

After

Renamed to "Social Communication Milestone Tracking" - flags absence of expected social behaviors without diagnostic claims. Added prominent disclaimers and liability protection language.

TW

Tom Wilson

Privacy Officer, HIPAA Consulting Group
"Baby video is incredibly sensitive data. You need to offer on-device processing options for privacy-conscious parents, even if cloud AI is more accurate. And your data retention policy of 'indefinite' is a legal nightmare waiting to happen."
Before

All video processed in cloud. Data retained indefinitely for AI training. No on-device option.

After

Hybrid architecture: On-device pose estimation (Edge AI), cloud only receives anonymized skeleton data. Video auto-deletes after 30 days. Opt-in for research data sharing with explicit consent.

Key Iteration Themes

1. Reduce Parental Anxiety, Don't Create It

Every expert emphasized that new parent anxiety is already at crisis levels. Our product must be reassuring by default, alerting only when truly necessary with clear, actionable guidance.

2. Clinical Accuracy vs. User Experience

Medical precision matters for credibility, but parent-friendly communication matters for adoption. We now maintain two "languages" - clinical for reports, conversational for daily use.

3. Privacy as a Feature, Not an Afterthought

Parents are increasingly savvy about data privacy. Our on-device processing option and transparent data policies became key differentiators, not just compliance checkboxes.

4. Integration with Existing Systems

Standalone apps fail. We pivoted to prioritize pediatrician EHR integration, state EI program partnerships, and baby monitor brand collaborations.
