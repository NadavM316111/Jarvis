Most Likely Test Topics

1. Antonina's Background

Her parents were shot in 1917 during the Russian Revolution. She was born in St. Petersburg in 1908. She studied music and languages, attended school in Tashkent at 15. Jan was 11 years older than her. They shared a white birch bed from their first apartment.

2. Lutz Heck's Back-Breeding Project

Lutz Heck was trying to recreate extinct animals like the aurochs (ancient cattle) and tarpan (wild horse) by selectively breeding modern animals. This connected to Nazi beliefs about racial purity - if you could bring back "pure" animals, the same logic could apply to humans.

3. The Pig Farm Plan

After Lutz Heck killed most of the zoo animals, Jan proposed converting the zoo into a pig farm. The pigs would be fed garbage collected from the Warsaw Ghetto. This was strategic - it gave Jan a legitimate reason to enter the ghetto regularly, which he used to help smuggle Jews to safety.

4. The Warsaw Zoo Before the War

One of the largest zoos in Europe, home to rare species. The villa had a stucco exterior with a peaked red tile roof. Rys could roam freely and play with baby animals. Antonina had an almost mystical connection with the animals.

5. The September 1939 Invasion

Germany invaded Poland on September 1, 1939. The zoo was bombed heavily. Some animals escaped, others were killed. Jan served as a lieutenant. Warsaw surrendered September 27. The eagle had to be shot because it was dangerous when loose.

6. Symbolism and Themes

Animals as innocents: Like the Jews who would be persecuted, animals are innocent victims of war.
The empty cages: Foreshadow their future use as hiding places.
Back-breeding: Parallels Nazi eugenics and racial ideology.
Antonina's empathy: Her ability to understand animals reflects her later compassion for refugees.
