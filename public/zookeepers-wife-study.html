The Zookeeper's Wife

Chapters 8-15 Study Guide | Diane Ackerman

Chapter 8: Lutz Heck and Nazi Ideology

This chapter provides a biography of Lutz Heck and explores Nazi ideology regarding eugenics and "animal purity." Heck is obsessed with resurrecting three "pure-blooded extinct species": tarpans, aurochsen, and the European bison. The Nazi obsession with animal purity foreshadows their horrific racial policies toward humans. Ironically, the Nazis considered themselves "ardent animal lovers" - a biologist was once punished for not giving worms enough anesthesia during an experiment.

Key Points:

  • Lutz Heck wants to resurrect extinct "pure" animal species
  • Nazi ideology connects animal purity to human racial purity
  • Paradox: Nazis claim to love animals while planning genocide

Chapter 9: Heck's Visit

Antonina wakes in darkness due to wartime blackouts - bedroom windows are sealed with plywood. She prepares for Lutz Heck's visit. Heck arrives appearing friendly and promises to persuade German high command to be lenient toward the zoo. However, he asks for the zoo's animals, saying "Please think of your animals as a loan." Antonina feels she has no choice but to comply with his request.

Key Points:

  • Wartime blackouts create constant darkness
  • Heck offers false friendship to get what he wants
  • The Zabińskis must surrender their animals

Chapter 10: The Betrayal

Heck reveals his true nature. He returns to the zoo with high-ranking SS friends to hunt the remaining animals - shooting them in their cages like a barbaric game. Antonina is shocked by Heck's betrayal after his earlier promises of protection. She begins to fear how many people will be "hunted" in the coming months, foreshadowing the horrors to come.

Key Points:

  • Heck's friendliness was deception
  • SS officers massacre zoo animals for sport
  • The animal hunt foreshadows Nazi persecution of Jews

Chapter 11: The Pig Farm

With the zoo animals gone, the property is converted into a pig farm. Jan gives Rys a pet piglet named Morys. Despite the grim circumstances, Antonina is happy her son has something resembling a normal childhood, even if his best friend is now a pig rather than exotic zoo animals.

Key Points:

  • The zoo transforms into a pig farm
  • Rys gets a pet piglet named Morys
  • Antonina tries to maintain normalcy for her son

Chapter 12: The Warsaw Ghetto

German hostilities escalate as over 400,000 Jews are locked into the Warsaw Ghetto. Friends Adam and Wanda Englert stage a fake divorce so Wanda can flee. At their farewell party, Antonina and Jan narrowly survive a bombing - saved only because they stopped to smoke. They rush to check on Rys's school but find all children safe. Wanda later reappears at the zoo disguised as Rys's non-Jewish tutor.

Key Points:

  • 400,000+ Jews confined to the Warsaw Ghetto
  • The Zabińskis survive a bombing by luck
  • Wanda assumes a false identity to survive
  • Theme: disguise and deception as survival tools

Chapter 13: Jan Joins the Underground

Jan feels "a moral indebtedness to the Jews" because he attended a primarily Jewish school. To repay this debt, he secretly uses the zoo to hide refugees. Working as part of the Polish Underground resistance, Jan assumes the code name "Francis" after Francis of Assisi, the patron saint of animals - a fitting name for a zookeeper turned savior.

Key Points:

  • Jan's Jewish school friends inspire his resistance work
  • The zoo becomes a hiding place for refugees
  • Jan's code name "Francis" honors his connection to animals
  • The Underground resistance network begins operating

Chapter 14: Hiding in Plain Sight

To camouflage their secret "Guests," Antonina and Jan keep the zoo populated with many legal visitors - hiding people in plain sight. Antonina becomes the hostess, managing extra chores and finding ways to provide food. Jan hides bomb-making materials underground. The biggest challenge is teaching young Rys to never speak about the Guests - if he does, everyone will be murdered. Tragically, German soldiers drag away Rys's beloved piglet Morys to be butchered.

Key Points:

  • Strategy: hide refugees among legitimate visitors
  • Antonina manages hospitality and extra food
  • Jan stores resistance weapons underground
  • Rys must keep deadly secrets at a young age
  • Morys the piglet is taken and killed

Chapter 15: The Beetle Connection

Germans shut down the pig farm, eliminating Jan's excuse for entering the Ghetto. The Nazis also destroy zoo plants and replace them with German species. Jan meets with Warsaw's Polish vice-president and obtains a Ghetto pass through the Parks and Gardens Department. This introduces him to famous entomologist Dr. Szymon Tenenbaum. Tenenbaum leaves his insect collection to Jan, which attracts a German officer named Ziegler who loves beetles. This connection gives Jan unprecedented Ghetto access to smuggle people out. When Tenenbaum dies, Ziegler leaves his dog Zarka with Antonina, and Jan plans to rescue Tenenbaum's widow.

Key Points:

  • Pig farm closure forces Jan to find new Ghetto access
  • Nazis replace Polish plants with German species (cultural erasure)
  • Dr. Tenenbaum's beetle collection becomes a rescue tool
  • Ziegler's beetle obsession unknowingly aids Jewish escape
  • Jan plans to save Tenenbaum's widow
